Our Story · Raisa & Brian

From a mountain, a mission

We are Raisa and Brian, the couple behind Kawsaypac. This is the story of how Ecuador changed our lives, and of the promise we made to the people and plants that changed them.

Begin the Story ↓

Why we do this

What started as a move to Ecuador became a calling: the plants that have kept families here well for generations deserve to be honored, not forgotten.

Raisa & Brian · Founders, Kawsaypac

Chapter One · Arriving

We came for the mountains. We stayed for the plants.

Moving to Ecuador was supposed to be a change of scenery. It became a change of everything. We woke beneath volcanoes that rose above the clouds, hiked gorges where waterfalls poured out of the cloud forest, and drank teas whose names we did not yet know.

In the markets we watched grandmothers sort bark and leaves by hand, speaking about plants the way you speak about old friends. We started asking questions. We have never stopped.

Chapter Two · Learning

The harvesters became our teachers

Our questions led us into the Andes and the Amazon, where Indigenous families have gathered these plants for generations. They showed us where each herb grows, when it is ready, and how to take only what the land can give.

We learned to harvest by hand, to dry in small batches, and to treat every plant as medicine rather than merchandise. It is slow work. It is the right work.

Chapter Three · Plant Wisdom

Sitting with a living tradition

The deeper we went, the clearer it became. Here, plants are not products. They are a language between people and the land, carried through ceremony, story and daily ritual.

So we sat, we listened, and we learned to respect the tradition long before we ever shared it. That respect still shapes every decision Kawsaypac makes.

Chapter Four · Kawsaypac

A name that means for life

Kawsaypac was born the way the best things are, slowly, and out of love. We built it around a simple belief: the plants that have kept Ecuadorian families well for generations deserve to be honored, not forgotten.

So we made a promise. Work directly with Indigenous harvesters. Pay fairly. Take only what the land can give. Put the whole herb in the pouch and the whole story on the label. This is medicine with a memory.

What we stand for

The promise in every pouch

We buy straight from the families who harvest, so the value stays with the communities who keep this knowledge alive.

Wildcrafted, not farmed

Gathered from the wild at the peak of potency, never grown in rows and never cut with fillers.
