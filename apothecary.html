Uncaria tomentosa

Cat's Claw

A climbing vine native to tropical forests of Central and South America. Its hooked thorns give the plant its common English name. Bark has a long history of use within Amazonian herbal traditions.

  • Habitat: humid lowland and foothill forest
  • Part traditionally used: inner bark
  • Common preparation: covered decoction
  • Sourcing lens: vine maturity and careful harvest matter

Passiflora species

Passionflower

A remarkable flowering vine whose intricate bloom is unmistakable. Leaves and aerial parts have traditionally appeared in gentle evening infusions across several herbal lineages.

  • Habitat: warm forest margins and cultivated gardens
  • Part traditionally used: leaf and aerial parts
  • Common preparation: hot infusion
  • Ritual context: often associated with winding down

Cinchona officinalis

Cinchona

An Andean tree with an important and complex history. Its bark has been known in Indigenous practice and later became central to global pharmaceutical history, which makes respectful sourcing and historical context essential.

  • Habitat: Andean cloud forest
  • Part historically used: bark
  • Preparation context: specialist knowledge is important
  • Stewardship lens: tree health and regeneration come first

Ilex guayusa

Guayusa

An evergreen holly native to the upper Amazon and cultivated in family gardens. Guayusa tea has a long social and cultural relationship with early morning gatherings in parts of Ecuador.

  • Habitat: upper Amazon gardens and forest edges
  • Part traditionally used: leaf
  • Common preparation: hot infusion
  • Natural characteristic: contains caffeine

Preparation language

Infusion or decoction?

The plant part often points toward the method. Always use the directions on the actual product.

Method 01

Infusion

Hot water is poured over softer plant material, then the vessel is covered while it steeps. Leaves and flowers often use this method.

  • Common for leaves and flowers
  • Covered steep helps hold aromatic compounds
  • Strain before drinking
Method 02

Decoction

Firmer plant material is gently simmered in water for a specified period. Roots, bark, and dense seeds may use this method.

  • Common for roots and bark
  • Gentle heat over a longer period
  • Follow exact time and quantity directions

Plant traditions vary by community and lineage. This archive is general education and does not replace product directions or care from a qualified healthcare professional.
