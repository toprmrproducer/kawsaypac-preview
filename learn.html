Tea preparation guide

A dependable cup begins with four small decisions.

Always follow the directions on your specific blend first. Use this as the framework around those instructions.

Read the pouch

Check the suggested amount, water temperature, steep time, and whether the blend is designed for daily or occasional use.

Measure with care

Use the listed serving size. More is not automatically better, especially when a blend contains concentrated roots or bark.

Match the method

Leaves and flowers are often infused in hot water. Firmer roots and bark may call for a gentle covered simmer.

Observe the ritual

Notice flavour, comfort, and how the practice fits your day. Consistency is more useful than intensity.

Preparing your body

Make space before you add another thing.

A useful herbal ritual is not a punishment or a dramatic reset. Begin with the basics: regular meals, adequate water, a consistent sleep window, and enough time to notice how a new practice feels.

Introduce one change at a time when possible. Keep the preparation simple, follow the product directions, and record any questions you want to discuss with a qualified professional.

  • Choose a repeatable time of day
  • Start with the label recommendation
  • Pair the ritual with food or water as directed
  • Pause if something feels wrong

Recipes + programs

Simple formats that make a ritual easier to keep.

Use these as flexible inspiration, then follow the exact directions on the blend you choose.

Evening ritualThe slow cup

Set a regular cut-off for screens, brew as directed, and give the cup a quiet place in the final hour of your day.

After mealsThe table reset

Keep the preparation uncomplicated and attach it to the meal you are most likely to eat at a consistent time.

Morning practiceThe first pour

Prepare your cup before the day becomes noisy, then take a moment to notice aroma and taste before moving on.

Weekly prepThe ritual tray

Keep the pouch, cup, spoon, and directions together so preparation asks for fewer decisions.
